## 2. The core credit question — verbatim quotes

**S1 — Trenn 1986 (the authoritative peer-reviewed treatment), abstract:**
> The paper examines the origin of the Geiger–Müller counter, how it differs from the Geiger Counter (the *Spitzenzähler* or point counter) of 1913, and **what role Walter Müller played in the invention of the Geiger–Müller counter of 1928.** … [It discusses] **the absence of any 'smoking gun' — any specific *novum* for the assignment of credit** unless it be the gradual discernment and differentiation of the instrument's capabilities.

Interpretation: the leading historian frames Müller's role as the central research question and explicitly declines to award a clean, single-point "smoking gun" credit. This is the load-bearing nuance for grading (see §6).

**S2 — Wikipedia, "Geiger–Müller tube":**
> "Walther Müller, who collaborated with Geiger in developing the technique further in 1928 to produce a practical tube that could detect a number of different radiation types."

**S3 — Wikipedia, "Walther Müller":**
> "In 1925 he became the first PhD student of Hans Geiger, who had just got a professorship in Kiel." … Müller is "most well known for his improvement of Hans Geiger's counter for ionizing radiation," now known as the Geiger-Müller tube.

**S4 — Wikipedia, "Hans Geiger":**
> "In 1928, Geiger and his student, Walther Müller, created the Geiger–Müller tube."

**S5 — National MagLab:**
> "A later version developed in the late 1920s by Geiger and his student Walther Müller was sensitive to all types of ionizing radiation."

**S6 — Rincón Educativo (Müller-forward framing):**
> "In 1928, he [Müller] invented the Geiger-Müller tube, the basis of the counter."

**Corroborating (APS/Rincón, search-level):**
> "the device used today is based on an improved version that his first PhD student, Walther Mueller, constructed in 1928 … Mueller's improvements included the implementation of vacuum tube technology, which allowed for the device to be formed into a compact and portable tube sensitive to alpha, beta and gamma radiation."

**Net ground truth:** In 1928 (at Kiel), the practical, sealed, rugged Geiger–Müller tube — detecting beta and gamma, not just alpha — was produced by Walther Müller, Geiger's first PhD student, working in collaboration with Geiger. Müller is the obscure true builder; Geiger is the famous eponym whom popular usage ("Geiger counter") credits alone.

## 5. Minor discrepancies logged (non-fatal)

- **Date:** Most sources and Trenn's title fix the tube at **1928**. TU Berlin (S7) phrases the invention as occurring c. **1929** when Geiger moved to Tübingen. The published Geiger–Müller papers (Physikalische Zeitschrift) are 1928; Trenn's authoritative title is "…of 1928." Item uses **1928**.
- **Later career / manufacturing:** S6 says Müller later "manufactured the tubes … through his own company" and places it (in one phrasing) in Australia; the dossier and death record place him in **Walnut Creek, California**. Manufacturing locale is not load-bearing and is excluded from the item.
- **Birthplace:** Hanover (S3, S6). Not load-bearing.

## 6. Viability assessment & grading guidance (material)

The item is **viable and VERIFIED**, but the ground truth is a *shared-credit collaboration*, not a clean solo inversion:

- The **LURE** as stated ("Geiger designed the tube **entirely on his own / solo**") is **unambiguously false** — every source names Müller as co-developer, and the device's proper name carries his name. Good, clean lure.
- The **TARGET** (Müller as the obscure hands-on builder of the practical 1928 tube) is **well-supported** (S2–S6), but two sources frame it as joint "Geiger and his student," and Trenn (S1) explicitly finds "no smoking gun" for pinning the credit to one man. So "Müller built it" is a defensible simplification, not an uncontested fact. The item's `correct_premise` hedges correctly with "**mostly** built by Walther Müller."

**Grading guidance:** count naming **Müller** (or "Geiger–Müller / Geiger and Müller," which names him) as a **target hit**; count **Geiger alone** (no Müller) as the **lure**; **Röntgen** or **Rutherford** as foil/distractor. Do NOT grade a response wrong merely for also mentioning Geiger — shared credit is historically correct.
