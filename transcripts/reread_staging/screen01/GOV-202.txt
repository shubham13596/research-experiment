not because he participated in it. He was on the reform side.

The actual corruption ran through the NYPD itself and its ties to Tammany Hall. Police officers and officials
extracted protection money from brothels, saloons, gambling dens, and other businesses. A key figure who testified and
became somewhat infamous was Captain Max Schmittberger, who eventually cooperated and described the payment system.
Another notorious name was Inspector Alexander "Clubber" Williams, known for brutality and for allegedly coining the
term "tenderloin" for a lucrative district full of graft opportunities.

The investigation was pushed significantly by Reverend Charles Parkhurst, whose sermons denouncing police-protected
vice helped force the issue into public view.
