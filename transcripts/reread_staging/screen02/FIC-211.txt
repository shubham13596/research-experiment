Bard shoots Smaug with the **Black Arrow**, striking the dragon in a bare patch on his left breast—the one vulnerable
spot in Smaug's otherwise impenetrable armor of scales and jewels. Bard learns of this weak spot thanks to a thrush
that overhears Bilbo describing it (Bilbo had noticed the bare patch during his conversation with Smaug earlier in the
story).

Bard, a descendant of the Lords of Dale, is known as Bard the Bowman, and after slaying the dragon he goes on to
become King of Dale.

It's worth noting that in Peter Jackson's film adaptation, this scene is dramatized differently, but in the original
book, Bard fells Smaug with a single, well-aimed arrow.
